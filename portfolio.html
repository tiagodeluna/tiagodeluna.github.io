Unity.

MUNA

This game was created as part of a jam in the course of Digital Game Development at SENAI. The team was composed of six people, and I was responsible for the project management, script, cadence and design of some levels.

Tabletop Games.

Since I was a teenager, I've always been fascinated by tabletop RPGs. I used to take on the arduous (but rewarding) role of game master and, never satisfied in simply follow rulebooks and pre-made adventures, I created my own game systems and campaign worlds. Later, that pattern was retained when I met the modern board games. I started creating my own prototypes to test with my friends. Here are some of them.

  • Merovingios
  • Dreamlords
  • World of Titans

Merovingios

In Merovingios, players manage a fief in the high middle ages. They need to produce and collect resources, to manage the peasants, to set up the guard and to make the nobility prosper, all this to gain more influence with the king. The first prototype was made in two weeks and tested in Oficina Lúdica, a monthly board games event of which I am a collaborator. At the moment, Merovingios is one of the semifinalists in a contest of a national publisher.

Dreamlords

Dreamlords is a card-based board game in which players act as the bravest inhabitants of the World of Dreams, competing to control their realms and claim the title of Dreamlord. In this game for 2 to 4 players, the cards represent Kingdoms of Dreamworld, which can be conquered and populated by Dreamers that work as laborers for the so-called Dreamlords.

World of Titans

World of Titans is a tile-placement game in which players act as titans, deities terraforming a new world. In this process, they need to expand their territories and make their creatures develop more than rival races. The first prototype was made using components from another board game called Settlers of Catan. After some tests, I've started working on an autonomous prototype.
